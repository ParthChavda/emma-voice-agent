How EMMA Works
Call Handling Process
What EMMA Does:

Answers every patient call instantly with no queues or hold times
Understands patient requests and triages appropriately
Books appointments by checking live availability
Handles appointment requests, test results, prescription renewals, and administrative queries
Captures patient details and interaction transcripts accurately
Routes calls to appropriate staff when needed
Transfers to human receptionists, GPs, or nurses immediately when required

What EMMA Does NOT Do:

Provide clinical advice or diagnose conditions
Make medical triage decisions
Handle medical emergencies directly
Transfer patients without context
Share or process patient data outside NHS systems
Make calls independently without patient initiation

Emergency & Escalation Protocols
Emergency Recognition & Routing:

Recognizes urgent situations immediately (chest pain, breathing difficulties, serious injuries, mental health crises)
Directs patients with emergencies to call 999 or 111 immediately
Transfers complex medical issues requiring clinical judgment to GPs/nurses instantly
Routes distressed or confused patients to human staff
Escalates calls that need medical assessment to appropriate clinicians with full context captured
Does not attempt to handle emergencies algorithmically

When EMMA Escalates:

Patient requests to speak to a person

Patient distress or confusion

Ambiguous or nuanced situations outside AI scope
